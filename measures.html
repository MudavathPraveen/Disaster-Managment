Earthquake Precautions

  • If you are indoors during an earthquake, drop, cover, and hold on. Get under a desk, table or bench. Hold on to one of the legs and cover your eyes. If there's no table or desk nearby, sit down against an interior wall. An interior wall is less likely to collapse than a wall on the outside shell of the building
  • Pick a safe place where things will not fall on you, away from windows, bookcases, or tall, heavy furniture.
  • It is dangerous to run outside when an earthquake happens because bricks, roofing, and other materials may fall from buildings during and immediately following earthquakes, injuring persons near the building.
  • Wait in your safe place until the shaking stops, then check to see if you are hurt. You will be better able to help others if you take care of yourself first, then check the people around you.
  • Move carefully and watch out for things that have fallen or broken, creating hazards. Be ready for additional earthquakes called "aftershocks."
  • Be on the lookout for fires. Fire is the most common earthquake related hazard, due to broken gas lines, damaged electrical lines or appliances, and previously contained fires or sparks being released.

Tsunami Precautions

  • Evacuate Immediately: Do not wait for an official warning. If you feel shaking in a coastal area or hear a tsunami warning, move to a safe place immediately.
  • Go to Higher Ground: Seek the nearest high ground or move as far inland as possible.
  • If Higher Ground Isn't Available: If you can't reach high ground, evacuate as far inland as possible. If that's not possible, go to a reinforced concrete or steel building and take shelter on the upper floors.
  • Stay Informed: Listen to local alerts and authorities for information on areas to avoid and shelter locations.
  • Avoid Traffic Jams: Evacuate on foot if possible to avoid traffic congestion.
  • Stay Away from Damage: Stay away from damaged buildings, roads, and bridges.
  • Don't Return: Do not leave the safe place until the warning is lifted. Do not return to the coastal area while a tsunami warning is in effect.
  • Take Your Emergency Kit: If possible, take your emergency kit with you, including important papers, medication, keys, mobile phone, and money.

Tornado Precautions

  • Stay informed by listening to weather updates and warnings.
  • Secure loose objects around your home like furniture and tools.
  • Keep emergency kits ready with essentials: food, water, flashlight, batteries, and first aid.
  • Evacuate immediately if advised by authorities.
  • Stay indoors, away from windows and glass doors during the cyclone.
  • Charge all electronic devices and keep backup power sources ready.
  • Avoid driving or walking through flooded areas.
  • After the cyclone, watch out for fallen power lines and unstable structures.

Thunderstorm

×

Thunderstorm Precautions

  • Stay indoors and avoid going outside during the storm.
  • Keep away from windows, doors, and anything that conducts electricity.
  • Unplug electrical appliances and avoid using wired electronics.
  • Avoid taking showers or baths, as plumbing can conduct electricity.
  • Stay away from tall objects like trees, poles, or towers.
  • If you're caught outside, crouch down in a low-lying area, keeping your feet together.
  • Do not lie flat on the ground as it increases the chance of being affected by ground current.
  • Stay inside until 30 minutes after the last clap of thunder.
  • Listen to weather updates and alerts on battery-powered devices.

Landslide Precautions

  • Stay alert during heavy rainfall or after earthquakes, especially if you live in a landslide-prone area.
  • Listen to weather alerts and warnings from local authorities.
  • Avoid building or living near steep slopes, drainage ways, or natural erosion valleys.
  • Inspect your property for signs of land movement like cracks in the soil or tilting trees and poles.
  • Have an emergency kit and evacuation plan ready for your family.
  • If you hear a rumbling noise or see debris flow, evacuate immediately to a safer location.
  • Stay away from the landslide path and do not cross flowing debris.
  • After a landslide, watch out for flooding and report any broken utility lines or damaged roads.
  • Help neighbors who may need assistance, especially the elderly and children.
